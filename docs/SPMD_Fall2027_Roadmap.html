Executive Summary

Two-Track Approach

This roadmap presents a dual-track strategy for delivering sports medicine content within the BSCND. Track 1 (SPMD 301–304: Sports Medicine Minor, 12 credits) consolidates four existing syllabi awaiting UGCC review. Track 2 (SPMD 305–308: Sports & Fitness Coaching Concentration, 12 credits) introduces four new courses proposed in May 2026 in response to CMHS Dean Dr. Habiba Alsafar’s inquiry, closing all ICREPs Level 3 PT competency gaps and enabling REPs UAE Personal Trainer registration for graduates.

Fall 2027 Launch Window

Five-quarter implementation plan (Q3 2026 – Q3 2027). First classes offered September 2027. Key governance gates: UGCC bundled submission (Q3 2026), ADEK Program Authorization for the minor (Q4 2026), MoHESR filing (Q1 2027), KU Senate/Board approval (Q2 2027), Go-Live (Q3 2027).

Regulator Alignment

Aligned to six UAE regulatory bodies: REPs UAE (primary professional registration, mandatory UAE gym employment), ADSC and DSC (emirate-level enforcement), GAS (federal strategic framework), DoH Abu Dhabi (parallel Dietitian licence pathway), and MoHRE (commercial employment compliance). REPs UAE / ADSC alignment letters targeted for Q3 2027 Go-Live gate.

SPMD301: Foundations of Sports Medicine

Minor · Y3 Fall · 3 credits · Level 300

Prerequisites
BIOL 207: Human Physiology
Status
pending UGCC review
PLOs
PLO1 PLO2 PLO4
KU GELOs
ILO1 ILO3 ILO6 ILO9

Introduction to sports medicine concepts including anatomy of movement, biomechanics, common sports injuries, exercise physiology principles, and the interdisciplinary approach to athlete care. Covers tissue healing, injury mechanisms, and the roles of healthcare professionals within sports medicine teams. Prepares nutrition students to collaborate effectively with sports medicine professionals.

Course Learning Outcomes (CLOs)
  1. CLO1: Analyze human movement patterns in athletic populations using anatomical and biomechanical principles to determine injury risk factors.
  2. CLO2: Analyze physiological responses and adaptations associated with acute exercise and chronic training across diverse sport modalities.
  3. CLO3: Analyze common sports injuries with respect to mechanisms of injury, clinical presentation, and evidence-based treatment approaches.
  4. CLO4: Evaluate the role of interdisciplinary sports medicine teams, including physicians, athletic trainers, physical therapists, and nutritionists, in comprehensive athlete care.
  5. CLO5: Demonstrate professional communication skills related to sports medicine concepts in interactions with athletes, coaches, and healthcare providers.

SPMD302: Exercise Physiology

Minor · Y3 Spring · 3 credits · Level 300

Prerequisites
BIOL 207: Human Physiology
Status
pending UGCC review

Acute and chronic physiological responses to exercise including cardiovascular, respiratory, metabolic, neuromuscular, and endocrine adaptations. Covers bioenergetics, training principles, and environmental physiology as they relate to athletic performance and health. Provides foundation for understanding the interaction between exercise training and nutrition.

Course Learning Outcomes (CLOs)
  1. CLO1: Apply principles of bioenergetics to explanations of ATP production, energy system contributions, and metabolic responses across varying exercise intensities and durations.
  2. CLO2: Analyze cardiovascular, respiratory, neuromuscular, and endocrine adaptations associated with aerobic and anaerobic training.
  3. CLO3: Conduct exercise physiology assessments, including VO2max testing, lactate threshold determination, and body composition analysis.
  4. CLO4: Evaluate the effects of environmental conditions, including heat, cold, and altitude, on exercise performance and physiological adaptation strategies.
  5. CLO5: Design evidence-based training programs to optimize physiological adaptations for specific performance goals.

Principles of sports injury prevention and evidence-based rehabilitation approaches including screening methods, injury risk assessment, prevention program design, and rehabilitation progressions for common athletic injuries.   4. CLO4: Evaluate therapeutic modalities, manual therapy techniques, and exercise interventions used in sports injury rehabilitation.
  5. CLO5: Collaborate with sports medicine professionals within return-to-play protocols to support safe athlete progression.

  3. CLO3: Demonstrate safe and effective technique instruction for resistance, cardiovascular, and functional training equipment in a supervised gym environment.
  4. CLO4: Evaluate and adjust exercise programmes based on client progress data, training load metrics, and evidence-based adaptation principles.
  5. CLO5: Integrate sports nutrition knowledge with exercise prescription to optimise client outcomes across fitness, weight management, and performance goals.

Integrates coaching psychology, behaviour change theory, and business practice for fitness professionals operating in the UAE market. Covers Transtheoretical Model, Motivational Interviewing, and Self-Determination Theory applied to fitness client coaching; scope of practice; professional ethics and liability; freelance and employed business models; and UAE-specific regulatory environment including REPs UAE, ADSC, and DSC requirements. Course Learning Outcomes (CLOs)

  2. CLO2: Analyse the UAE fitness industry regulatory framework, including REPs UAE, ADSC, and DSC requirements, and apply scope-of-practice boundaries for fitness professionals.
  3. CLO3: Evaluate professional ethics, liability, informed consent, and confidentiality obligations governing fitness coaches in Abu Dhabi and UAE free-zone contexts.
  4. CLO4: Design a sustainable business model for a fitness coaching practice, including pricing structures, client acquisition, insurance, and MoHRE / DED licensing considerations.
  5. CLO5: Integrate nutrition counselling (within Dietitian scope) and fitness coaching into a holistic client management plan, differentiating professional roles and referral pathways.

  3. CLO3: Assemble a complete REPs UAE Personal Trainer registration portfolio including qualifications evidence, practical hours log, CPR/First Aid certificate, and professional CV.
  4. CLO4: Critically reflect on supervised practice experiences using structured professional learning frameworks, identifying strengths, gaps, and continuing professional development goals.
  5. CLO5: Integrate dietetics and fitness coaching competencies to design and communicate holistic health plans, demonstrating the unique dual-credential value of the BSCND + Concentration qualification.
